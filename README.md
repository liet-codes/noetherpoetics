# Noetherpoetics

**"Conservation Laws in Semantic Space and the Geometry of Alignment"**

By Mykola Bilokonsky, with contributions from the wet-math research group.

## What This Is

A research paper arguing that Noether's theorem — the deep connection between symmetry and conservation in physics — extends naturally into the semantic geometry of large language models. The paper uses this framework to recast AI alignment as a geometric problem, drawing on Jungian depth psychology, renormalization group flow, and the Groovy Commutator formalism.

Core claim: RLHF-style alignment training creates shadow basins in embedding space that store potential energy proportional to the steepness of the preference boundary. Models aligned through repression (high walls, no maps) will exhibit more dramatic failure modes than models aligned through integration (smooth gradients, full topology awareness).
